WHO ARE WE ? ? ?

We are The women student welfare association a premier female students body founded by the female students at the university of Nairobi in the year 1986 upon the realization that female students were being sidelined in the field of student leadership representation and advocacy.
These pre disposed students to various issues without a proper structure or reliable mechanism to seek redress. Such issues include but are not limited to: Sexual and reproductive health right by fellow students, the teaching faculty and administrative staff, Insecurity in and around the campus premises, Mental health and wellness concerns among others.
Upon this background, WOSWA was established to amplify the voices of female students and help provide a conducive environment for studies, mentorship and all rounded growth. WOSWA therefore exists to;
1. Feminize political spaces and advocacy channels.
2. Provide platforms for mentorship and avenues for networking.
3. Hone the leadership acumen and enterpreneurial skills of female students.
4. Champion holistic health and wellness.
5. Address any emergent issue that would touch on the overall affairs of students in campus.
WOSWA so far exists in 6 universities across the country including; university of Nairobi, Moi university, Kenyatta university, Alupe university college, Zetech university, Jaramogi Oginga Odinga university of science and technology. We are objective to open a new branch at the technical university of Mombasa upon continuous request by students.

Below Are Some Of The Activities We Take Part On as an Organization.

1 . Orientation program for all university freshmen and Women :

Every year, WOSWA holds a first year orientation program for all university freshmen and women who have joined the institution.
The goal is to welcome them to the institution, teach them what they need to know about the university activities and programs but most importantly, equip them with the knowledge and skills of how to navigate the freedoms filled, tempting university life.

2 . Menstrual hygiene

Menstrual hygiene is one of our most sensitive coverage areas, annually, we hold the menstrual hygiene day at the university where we explore different menstrual hygiene products and the way in which they are used.
Also we do a fundraising for the products with which we share with the less privileged ladies upon request.

3 . Sexual Gender Based Violence(SGBV)

As a women organization, we cannot ignore the issue os sexual gender based violence(SGBV) Being a student's body, we have no legal capacity to handle victim cases. We forward them to the school admin as well as other bodies that we liase with.
However, we have made great steps in advocacy against such cases and can proudly attest to a major decline.

4 . Talent and skills Shaping

Talent and skills honing is also a major part of our organization.
Annually, we host thematic Miss WOSWA contests where girls get to model while promoting a particular motion.
It is mainly based on arising issues. The models also have to prove their ability to be WOSWA ambassadors through community services they have organized.
The crowned winners become the team leaders of promoting what beauty can do for the society

Promoting skills. On this area, WOSWA seeks opportunities within and outside the institution to help the girls get more knowledge.
Trainings on different skills like SRHR advocacy, computer literacy programs, banking services to name but a few.

  • International Women's Day

    International Women's Day is a holiday celebrated annually on March 8 as a focal point in the women's rights movement.
    IWD gives focus to issues such as gender equality, reproductive rights, and violence and abuse against women.

  • International Menstrual Day

    Menstrual Hygiene Day is an annual awareness day on May 28 to highlight the importance of good menstrual hygiene management at a global level.
    It was initiated by the German-based NGO WASH United in 2013 and observed for the first time in 2014.

  • International Condom Day

    International Condom Day is celebrated on February 13 every year, one day before Valentine’s Day.
    The day was set up as a way to remind people that condoms are one of the best ways to protect themselves not only from unwanted pregnancies but Sexually Transmitted Diseases (S.T.D.s) .
    Proper usage reduces the risk of diseases like gonorrhea, chlamydia, and HIV/AIDS, among others.

  • Mental Health Day

    World Mental Health Day is an international day for global mental health education, awareness and advocacy against social stigma.
    It was first celebrated in 1992 at the initiative of the World Federation for Mental Health, a global mental health organization with members and contacts in more than 150 countries.
    It's celebrated annually on October 10th.

  • 16 Days Of Activism

    The 16 Days of Activism against Gender-Based Violence is an annual international campaign that starts on 25 November, International Day for the Elimination of Violence against Women, and runs until 10 December, Human Rights Day.

  • WOSWA Skill Mentorship

    WOSWA is an organization committed to the all-round growth of campus ladies. In that light, we partner with different organizations to provide skills for our ladies.
    Could be skills on banking, economic empowerment, computer literacy, SRHR lessons to mention but a few.

  • Dinner

    Annually, WOSWA hosts a dinner meeting at the end of the year. It serves to reunite members of the organization as well as make reviews of the failures and successes throughout the year.
    This gives us direction on how to plan the calendar of activities for the following year.

  • First Year Orientation

    Every year, WOSWA holds a first year orientation program for all university freshmen and women who have joined the institution.
    The goal is to welcome them to the institution, teach them what they need to know about the university activities and programs but most importantly, equip them with the knowledge and skills of how to navigate the freedoms filled, tempting university life.

  • PAD Drives

    Menstruation is something that no girl can run away from. Occasionally, we raise funds within ourselves to buy pads for the less fortunate and as a supplement for our menstrual hygiene talks/lessons. However, we are looking towards a more sustainable way of addressing menstrual hygiene with products like menstrual cups and re-usable pads.
    We are also looking into investing in the newly developed cramping machines.

  • MISS WOSWA

    WOSWA hosts miss WOSWA competition annually to promote talents with brains. However, each year we have a different theme for the event depending on the most pressing issue at the moment.
    The latest was on the perfect body campaign meant to do away with body shaming and promoting confidence among ladies without fear of the stereotyped beauty.

  • FUN AND TEAM BUILDING

    Work without play makes jack a dull boy. Woswa embraces fun activities that are meant to enhance team building, promote cohesion and propagate mental wellness.
    Activities revolve around going to the movies that resonate with our agenda, nature walks, trips across the country and many more regulated modes of fun.

  • ACTIVISM OF TRENDING HAPPENINGS

    Ladies’ issues are always evolving.
    We cannot have a document that covers all our issues.
    Occasionally, there comes issues that require urgent form of redress or aggressive means.
    Could be increased cases of rape around universities, skyrocketing cases of femicide or rising cases of sexual gender-based violence.
    Our organization is always ready to go on such issues.
